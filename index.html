How to Make a Quesadilla

Joshua Popp

Overview:

Quesadillas are one of the most popular dishes served in mexican house holds. This is due to the cheap price and simplicity of making them. A quesadilla can be compared to a classic grilled cheese sandwich; however, instead of bread and american cheese, one uses tortillas and monteray jack cheese

Ingredients:

  • 2 tortillas
  • 1/2 cup Monteray Jack cheese
  • 1 tbsp. salsa of your choice

Step 1: Prepare the workspace

To begin it is important to prepare the workspace to make the cooking process easier and more organized.

Prepare a serving plate and move all the ingredients near the stove, since the assembly occurs in the pan. Make sure to get a spoon for adding the salsa to the finished quesadilla.

Note: if you are planning to add meat to the quesadilla cook it before adding it to the quesadilla, since the cooking process is short and won't cook the meat before serving.

Step 2: Heat a pan

Move a small sized pan on to the stove, if you have a traditional comal that will work aswell.

Turn the stove to medium heat and wait for the pan to the heat up. Use medium heat so that there is enough heat to melt the cheese, but to not burn the tortilla while it cooks.

WARNING: Do not add oil to the pan, as it will fry the quesadilla and cause it to be soggy in the end.

Step 3: Assemble the quesadilla

Place the first tortilla in the pan, if it begins to bubble instantly turn down the heat.

Quickly grab the cheese and sprinkle it onto the tortilla, make sure that the cheese is spread evenly so that all melts the same.

Next, grab the second tortilla and place it on top of the cheese.

WARNING: Do not flip the tortilla in this step, since the cheese has not melted yet.

Step 4: Cook the quesadilla

Cook the tortilla on the first side for around 3 - 4 Minutes. Before flipping check to make sure the cheese is melting and has connected to the tortillas.

Grab a spatula and scoop under the tortilla and carefully flip it to the other side. If there are small black burn marks then the tortilla has cooked for long enough. If there are none of these marks cook it on that side for an extra minute.

Repeat step 4 on the other side.

Note: All stoves are different so cook times will vary

Step 5: Finish & Enjoy

Once both sides of the quesadilla have small black burn marks then the quesadilla is read to be served.

Use the spatula to pick up the quesadilla and move onto the serving plate. Wait 1 minute before adding the salsa, since the cheese will be hot to the touch.

Open the quesadilla and add a tablespoon of salsa to the quesadilla. Close it, and serve the finished product.

Note: You can add more salsa if you desire.
